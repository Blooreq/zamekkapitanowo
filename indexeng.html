About Castle Kapitanowo

The origins of the castle are linked to the construction of a 14th-century donjon,which was the main part of the establishment. Over the centuries the object underwent significant reconstructions,gaining another tower with residential functions and a closed inner courtyard as early as the 15th century.The 16th century and the first half of the 17th century brought further development of the castle in the spirit ofthe Renaissance era. During the Baroque period it underwent partial decorative changes.The building was in use until the 1990s, later abandoned it succumbed to devastation.

Exploring

Exploring in night

We invite you to visit in an unconventional way. Castle guests, accompanied by an expert guide, will have the opportunity for an experiential encounter both with the castle walls and with numerous artifacts discovered during the research of the object - for enthusiasts a unique attraction will be the opportunity to get their hands on some of them. We organize unusual history lessons for both adults and school children. The turbulent history of the castle stretched over seven centuries provides a wonderful canvas for historical stories ranging from defensive architecture to the daily life of the castle.

At night the castle takes on a mysterious atmosphere. Enjoy an unforgettable tour with candles in your hands, during which you will discover secrets hidden in dark chambers. Feel the thrill and let yourself be swept away by the stories of the castle's former inhabitants. Night tours are a unique opportunity to stimulate your imagination and see the Castle from a completely different side. You will learn about its rich history, legends and secrets that hide its walls. We invite both organized groups and individual tourists. Night sightseeing is an ideal option for those wishing to experience something special and unique.

CASTLE HISTORY

Kapitanowo Castle is a 14th-century knight's castle that has been expanded and changed owners over the centuries. For a while it was an important center of the region, but in the 19th century it began to decline. After World War II it fell into disrepair, until it was finally bought back by a private owner and restored. Today it is one of the popular tourist attractions in the Kłodzko region.
